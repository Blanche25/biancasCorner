jamiep when i get you jamiep

22nd aug 2025

i think you should go listen to Constant Companions (Deluxe Edition). pretty please? prettiest of pleases? i listened to it and i mean oh my god. oh my fucking god.

is this blog post just an excuse to gush about music i like under the pretense of a review?
yes, yes it is.

so! this isn't going to be a review of every single song in the album, i'm just gonna go over things that i particularly liked about the entire thing and maybe make some examples along the way.

first, the obvious: Constant Companions (Deluxe Edition) is Jamie Paige's latest album, and it exists as a more complete version of the original Constant Companion, as explained by Jamie herself.
it has 5 extra songs, 4 of them at the end of the album and one of them, Clouddrop, inbetween already existing songs.

Constant Companions was already a banger, easily my favorite Jamie Paige album and among my favorites of all time. But my god the extra tracks on this one elevate it to heights never before thought possible by mere mortals.
but, you may be asking yourself, my blissfully ignorant reader, "Bianca, what the fuck are you on about?" and i am LITERALLY about to tell you my god can you like wait a minute?????

first off i think is the music itself, every song is rich in every possible aspect, from melody to harmony to instrumentation, though the latter isn't exactly my area of expertise. each song has so much going on, and the developments aren't always so predictable as one would expect.
also the leitmotifs, there is just so many leitmotifs in every single track. There's the obvious Constant Companions leitmotif, which is basically the "baby do you know what you wanna hear?" line which has been burned into my cortex i hear it in my sleep. but also so many tracks have their own leitmotifs that appear in OTHER tracks and everything just plays all at once in "Dance Delightful", the album's last track which serves as an amazing conclusion and also as a sort of recap of all previous tracks.

harmonically there's also a lot to talk about, i like fancy chords and weird lil' progressions, and there's plenty here, so needless to say my thirst was sated. though i'm not gonna go into too much detail, since i definitely am not doing a harmonic analysis of every single song on the album. yet.
i absolutely loved the lyrics of every track, which isn't something i say often, since i find it's rare that i get particularly swept away by a song's lyrics, but once again Jamie Paige makes me die just thinking about her lyrics (in a good way i promise).
one of the most interesting ones in that regard, at least in my opinion, is Object of Affection. the lyrics in that one make me go insane. i really liked the more ornate language used, as i think it's definitely not a common style in songs, and apparently, according to a tumblr post by Jamie, it's about a trans prince that gets turned into a doll which sounds sick as hell honestly.

Clouddrop is easily my favorite song in the entire album, possibly my favorite ever. it's the "seven minute long behemoth" that was originally cut from Constant Companions.
not gonna talk too much about it, but it's a great song, the lyrics are insane, so is the music, just go listen to it.



ok i think that's enough yapping, go listen to Jamie's stuff! bye!
