doomscrolling and such

19th jul 2025

i've always kinda had problems with my screentime, worsened by abysmal ability to focus on tasks (or viceversa, tbf).
the time i spend daily on stuff like youtube or instagram or whatever else i'd rather spend doing literally anything else more fulfilling, i have many hobbies that i unfortunately tend to neglect, making music, coding, journaling, reading and etc.
the thing about spending so much time scrolling is that i didn't even like it. every time i close out whichever app i was in i'm like "well that was a waste of time, i learned nothing and actually i feel worse than i did before",

so i think something like a year ago i decided to delete most stuff on my phone, except maybe youtube because i am weak, in an attempt to fix my problem. definitely didn't work, but it was a first step, so sometime after that i started putting screentime limits on a bunch of apps, which worked a lot better for keeping me off doomscrolling, though sometimes i still temporarily removed those limits because god forbid i miss my 4 hours of daily youtube.

still, wanting to go further, i started putting limits on websites on my pc as well, and i tried to get myself to write more in my diary and do stuff i actually wanted to do, which, to be fair, worked kinda ok, i wrote an original piece for guitar, started doing a lot of worldbuilding for a d&d campaign i want to run, and i made a website! i even got to playing more video games i wanted to try, which was a lot more difficult when the option of being entertained for no effort was right there and also free. now every time i pick up my phone i can actually use it for what i inteded to instead of getting sucked into hell, which i think is pretty cool.

i did notice while doing all of this, that besides mindless entertainment, i did actually rely on social media for some things, mainly recommendations of other media, like video games and music.
thankfully, this turned out to actually be a good thing! i did always think it was not great for me to just absorb the tastes of everyone i follow online, and the other methods for discovering new stuff are, i think, a lot better anyways.
one thing that helped me is this video by Notes From The Archives, in which he talks about how he discovers new music without social media.

sooo yeah that's about it, recently i've been into self improvement stuff and actually making myself a better person, and i'm pretty happy with the progress i made so far, even if it's not that much in perspective.
